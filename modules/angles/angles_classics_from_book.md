## Child-Friendly Learning Spine

1. Do not guess the angle. Name the move.
2. Straight line means `180°`.
3. Triangle means `180°`.
4. Isosceles means two matching base angles.
5. Parallel lines give three relation types:
   - Z-angle: equal
   - F-angle: equal
   - C-angle: add to `180°`
6. Parallel-zigzag questions may ask for `a + b`, not just one angle.
7. Competition questions usually chain two or three moves.
8. Equilateral triangles quietly give three `60°` angles.
9. Polygon totals use `(n - 2) x 180°`; regular polygons then divide by `n`.

## Common Misconceptions

- Treating all angles in an isosceles triangle as equal.
- Forgetting that an exterior angle may need `180° - outside angle`.
- Mixing up co-interior angles with equal angles.
- Treating `a + b` questions as if only one angle is being asked.
- Using the triangle sum before converting the shown outside angle into the inside angle.
- Using a polygon's full interior-angle sum as one regular angle.
- Using `360° / n` as the only polygon method and missing `(n - 2) x 180°`.
