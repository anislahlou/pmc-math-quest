## Lesson Objectives

- Calculate the volume of prisms, hollow objects, and composite objects.
- Solve for the water level when an object is fully submerged.
- Solve for the water level when an object is partially submerged.

## Named Module Classics

1. **Cuboid Warmup** - direct and reverse use of `length x width x height`.
2. **Base Area Stack** - any prism volume from `base area x height`, before shape-specific formulas.
3. **Hollow Prism** - outer cross-section minus hole cross-section, then multiply by height.
4. **Hollow Height** - reverse the hollow-prism formula to find the prism height.
5. **Composite Cross-Section** - split the cross-section into rectangles and triangles, then multiply by prism length.
6. **Water Rise Volume** - fully submerged volume from water-height increase.
7. **New Water Level** - known submerged volume becomes a height increase.
8. **Full Tank Removal** - tank filled while object is inside, then object removed.
9. **Partial Submerged Block** - tall vertical block sticks out; water only has the remaining base area to rise through.
10. **Extensive Cross-Section** - square-plus-trapezium prism from the optional challenge page.

## Diagram Fidelity Requirements

- Dimension labels must sit on the exact edge or height they describe.
- Hollow-prism diagrams must show the top hole and separate outer-side and hole-side labels.
- Composite prisms must show the cross-section split, not just a generic cuboid.
- Water diagrams must show before/after levels and the change in height.
- Partial-submerged diagrams must visibly show a tall block protruding above the water.
- Worked states must show the formula bridge, not only the final answer.

## Variation Rule

The exercise bank keeps the same mathematical structures from the source but generates fresh numbers. This avoids children answering by memorising the printed examples while preserving the study-book question shapes.
