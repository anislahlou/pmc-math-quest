# Volume of Prisms Intro Video Pack

Module: Volume of Prisms

Source: `Y6-M-L10-L18-Study Book.pdf`, Lesson 15, book pages 84-98 / PDF pages 94-108.

## Learning Outcome

After the intro, the learner should be able to see a prism as a stack of identical cross-sections, then use that idea for hollow objects, composite cross-sections, and water-displacement problems.

## Named Skills Shown Before Practice

- Base Area Stack
- Hollow Prism
- Composite Cross-Section
- Water Rise Volume
- New Water Level
- Full Tank Removal
- Partial Submerged Block
- Extensive Cross-Section

## Scene Storyboard

| Scene | Title | Purpose | Child-facing voiceover |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | A Prism Is A Stack | Show the repeated cross-section before the formula. | "A prism is like the same slice copied again and again. If we know one slice, we can stack it through the height." |
| 2 | Base Area x Height | Bridge the visual stack to the formula. | "The base area tells us one layer. The height tells us how many layers. That is why volume is base area times height." |
| 3 | Hollow Means Subtract | Preserve the source hollow-prism form. | "A hole is missing space. Find the outside volume, find the hole volume, then subtract." |
| 4 | Split The Cross-Section | Prepare rectangle-plus-triangle and L-shape prisms. | "For a composite prism, do not panic. Split the front face into friendly shapes, add their areas, then stretch that area through the prism." |
| 5 | Water Rise Is Volume | Show fully submerged displacement. | "When a stone goes fully under water, the extra water height tells us exactly how much space the stone took." |
| 6 | Known Volume Raises Water | Reverse the displacement relationship. | "If we already know the stone volume, divide it by the base area. That gives the water rise." |
| 7 | Full Then Remove | Explain the filled-tank removal pattern. | "If the tank was full while the object was inside, taking the object out leaves a missing chunk of water equal to the object's volume." |
| 8 | Tall Block Trap | Introduce partial submergence. | "If the block sticks out, only the underwater part matters. The water has less floor space to occupy, so we use the container area minus the block area." |
| 9 | Ready To Train | Tie intro skills to practice. | "Now you will meet the same moves with fresh numbers, diagrams, hints, and repair questions if one catches you." |

## Warmup Gate

1. Cuboid volume from three dimensions.
2. Prism volume from base area and height.
3. Hollow prism volume from outside area minus hole area.
4. Water displacement from base area and height change.

## Misconception Risks

- Multiplying all visible numbers even when a hole should be subtracted.
- Using total tank height instead of water-height change.
- Forgetting to divide by the number of equal stones.
- Adding the entire tall block volume when the block is only partly submerged.
- Treating a composite cross-section as a full rectangle.

## NotebookLM Steering Prompt

Create a short explainer for an 11-year-old learning Volume of Prisms from Think Academy Lesson 15. Use only the supplied lesson pages. Start with the visual idea that a prism is the same cross-section stacked through a height. Then introduce `volume = base area x height`. Cover hollow prisms by subtracting the missing hole area, composite prisms by splitting the cross-section, and water-displacement problems by connecting volume to water-level change. Include the challenge case where a tall block is only partly submerged, so the water rises in the container base area minus the block base area. Keep the tone warm and concrete. End by telling the learner they will train with fresh numbers, mixed answer formats, hints, worked solutions, and similar repair questions after mistakes.
