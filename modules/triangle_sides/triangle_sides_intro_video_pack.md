# Triangle Sides Intro Video Pack

Mission: Lesson 17 - Relationship Between Sides In Triangles

## Learning Outcome

By the end of the intro, the learner should be able to decide whether side lengths can make a triangle, then use right-triangle and isosceles relationships to find missing sides, perimeters, and areas.

## Named Skills Shown Before Practice

- Triangle Gate
- Isosceles Choice
- Hypotenuse Builder
- Missing Leg
- Shared Height Chase
- Isosceles Split Area
- Area To Perimeter
- Right-Turn Path

## Storyboard

1. **Can It Close?**
   Show three sticks. The two shorter sticks must be long enough to meet over the longest stick.

2. **The Triangle Gate**
   Put the lengths in order. If the two short sides add to more than the longest side, the triangle can close.

3. **The Right-Angle Promise**
   Show a right triangle and label the side opposite the square corner as the hypotenuse.

4. **Build The Hypotenuse**
   Use `a^2 + b^2 = c^2` with a clean triple such as `6, 8, 10`.

5. **Find A Missing Leg**
   Reverse the same rule: subtract the known square from the hypotenuse square.

6. **Shared Height Chase**
   Drop one height inside a larger triangle. Use two smaller right triangles to find hidden base pieces.

7. **Isosceles Split**
   Split an isosceles triangle down the middle. The base is halved; Pythagoras finds the height.

8. **Reverse From Area**
   Use area and base to find height first, then use the split triangle to find equal sides.

9. **Right-Turn Challenge**
   Combine alternating horizontal and vertical moves, then use one final right triangle to find the greatest distance.

## Warmup Gate

Before full practice, the learner should answer:

1. Can `4, 5, 6` form a triangle?
2. What is the hypotenuse if the legs are `6` and `8`?
3. What is the missing leg if the hypotenuse is `13` and the other leg is `5`?
4. In an isosceles triangle with equal sides `13` and base `10`, what is the height?

## Voiceover Direction

Keep it concrete: "Can the sticks close?", "Which side is opposite the right angle?", "What square do we subtract?", "Why does the base split in half?"

Do not open with formula memorisation. The visual reason comes first, then the formula.
