## Named Module Classics

1. **Triangle Gate** - decide whether three lengths can form a triangle using the rule that the two shorter sides must add to more than the longest side.
2. **Isosceles Choice** - two side lengths are given; decide which length must be the repeated side so the triangle is possible, then find the perimeter.
3. **Hypotenuse Builder** - find the hypotenuse from two perpendicular legs.
4. **Missing Leg** - find a missing perpendicular side from the hypotenuse and the other leg.
5. **Shared Height Chase** - use two right triangles sharing an altitude to find a base, perimeter, or area.
6. **Isosceles Split Area** - split an isosceles triangle down the middle, use Pythagoras to get height, then find area.
7. **Area To Perimeter** - reverse from area and base to height, then use Pythagoras to find equal sides and perimeter.
8. **Right-Turn Path** - group alternating 90-degree moves into two perpendicular totals, then use Pythagoras.

## Core Rules

- Triangle inequality: for side lengths `a <= b <= c`, a triangle exists only if `a + b > c`.
- Pythagorean Theorem: in a right triangle, `a^2 + b^2 = c^2`, where `c` is the hypotenuse.
- Missing leg: `leg = sqrt(hypotenuse^2 - other leg^2)`.
- Isosceles altitude: the altitude from the top vertex splits the base into two equal halves.
- Area of a triangle: `area = base x height / 2`.

## Diagram Fidelity Requirements

- Right-angle marks must sit at the actual perpendicular corner.
- The hypotenuse must be visually opposite the right angle and never labelled as a leg.
- In split-height diagrams, the foot of the altitude must be named or visibly on the base.
- In isosceles diagrams, equal sides must look equal and the base split must be shown when area is tested.
- Triangle inequality questions should show the longest side as the side being tested against the sum of the other two.
- The right-turn path challenge must show the path as alternating perpendicular moves, not as a straight-line addition problem.

The app preserves the source structures while generating fresh integer triples and clean Pythagorean cases. This keeps the skill faithful to the lesson while preventing children from memorising the printed examples.
